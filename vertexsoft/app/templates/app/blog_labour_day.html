Labour Day

Labour Day is a day that celebrates the rights empowerment of the people who works and earn their livelihood. Labour Day supports the labour union movement .this day is celebrated to aware the people about the hard works that is incurred by labourers in their society. A society runs on the support of many pillars and labourers are one of the main pillars that provides ultimate strength for the growth and development of a society. By celebrating Labour Day on yearly bases we pay a tribute to the workers for the achievements and well-being for our country.

The origination of Labour Day

In most of the country Labour Day is observed on every 1st of May but the celebration date varies in some countries like Australia Bangladesh and Canada. In most countries the Labour Day is termed as “international Workers day” the main purpose of celebrating Labour Day is also to ensure the 8 hours working in a day. By this labour movement the social activist wanted to ensure that the labourers in every field of life should work only for 8 hours in a day with no less of pay.

Many movements and protest were organized and supported for the enforcement of the these two deserved rights of the workers and somehow they succeeded and laws are established in this regard.

In many regions of the world the labour law is being followed .there is a contradiction to name the people who actually originated and came up with the idea of celebrating the Labour Day worldwide.

Labour law in Pakistan and its enforcement

  • There are many laws are observed in Pakistan to support the workers like

  • Employment of children act

  • Control of employment ordinance 1965

  • Employees cost of living Relief Act

  • Apprenticeship ordinance and so on.

Pakistan is the 6th most populated country in the world and its estimated labour force is 51.78 million. In labour forces we can consider all the persons from the age of 10 to onward who work to earn their livelihood whether they are employed or unemployed. According to the estimation the employed labour force is 40.09 million and most of them are in rural areas. As we have labour law in Pakistan ,we also have a predefined labour judiciary. In Pakistan labour day is also celebrated on 1st May of every year and many movements are running for the betterment and rights protection for workers but the actual condition is pretty different. No matter the rules are devised and the day is also being celebrated but the main reason of all these activities is not yet materialized. There are still child labours in Pakistan. The people are still working for whole day to get paid for very small amount. There is a complete violation of labours laws are happening in our country. No facilities’ are provided to the worker especially for those who work on daily wages. They are living their lives under the lines of poverty. Days are celebrated, movements are running in their support but the actual aim is not yet achieved and the worst thing is that nobody cares about it not even the officials of our country. Every time the governments change, these workers are promised to get a better life and working facilities but all in vain as usual. We can change this mind set of our ruling persons if we change ours first. Small continuous efforts always results in big out comes. As the change always starts from one’s own home. If one can respect and ensure the right of every worker who they have in their life then gradually we can change the society norms also.
